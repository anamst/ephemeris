OUR VISION

A Vibrant Space Economy
Tokenization is the key to making space available to everyone.
A space econonomy where governments and Big Tech can't easily impose total control over resources.
A decentralized network of ground stations, storage and computing nodes which helps the space industry grow.
An infrastructure that enables a global space market while preserving the privacy of data and the activities.
A framework where space companies, agencies and users can monetize their products and services fairly.

THE CHALLENGE

Space is not for everyone.

Centralized control & offerings.

Private space companies have somewhat changed the industry. But most means to build space platforms or provide related services remain highly centralized or rigidly brokered.

Sub-optimal market exposure.

Marketplaces for space goods and services are still few and far between. Some of the most useful space products, like Earth Observation data, lack adequate exposure to demand.

Previous-century rules & flows.

The government origins of space have left a legacy of tight regulation and engineering overhead. Enterprises must evolve, but without sacrificing privacy, security and quality.

How Do We Achieve It?
New tools for a new era.
Enabling space data marketplaces.

Reliable global marketplaces for space data will bring huge benfits for Earth's economy. The planet's population is growing, and with it the need to become more efficient. Straightforward access to space data can power agriculture, urban development, climate science, security, transportation and weather services for the benefit of everyone.

A user-centric market infrastructure.

Products and services can be developed by anything from small university teams to government institutes and major private corporations. They must be able to interact directly while maximizing the value and protecting the intellectual value of their work.

Faster and cheaper space missions.

The key to mass-scale spaceflight is in lower costs - through standardization and wide participation. A planetary-scale ground segment will allow space enterprises to reduce the deployment time of their space missions. To build this global infrastructure, new models of trust and automation are needed.
